Stacked Bar for Batting Averages of Baseball Players vs Weight and Height

labels notes:
weight labels['light','midlight','medium','midheavy','heavy'] represent 5 even segments from 140~245 pounds
height labels ['Short','Medium','Tall'] represent 3 even segments from 65~80 inches
Batting Averages labels ['low','mid','high'] represent 3 even segments from 0.000~0.328 inches

data set containing 1,157 baseball players including height (in inches), weight (in pounds), batting average, and home runs

  • value table and corresponding labels are in the table above
  • the X-axis represent the weight and height groups of baseball players
  • the y-axis represent the number(or percent) of people have low , mid , high level of batting average in each group
  • You can move mouse over the rectangles to see deatiled information .

Based on this visualization we can know:

  • players with high height tend to have bad avg
  • most baseball players have weights between 160~200 pound and 70~75 inchs.
